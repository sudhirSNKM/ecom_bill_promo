GDPR Compliance

Last Updated: March 1, 2026

Zenith is committed to full compliance with the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). This page explains how GDPR applies to your use of the Zenith platform and outlines the rights available to EU/EEA data subjects.

Overview

The GDPR is a comprehensive EU data protection law that governs how organizations collect, use, and store personal data of individuals in the European Union and European Economic Area. Zenith processes personal data as both a Data Controller (for platform user accounts) and a Data Processor (for customer data managed by merchants using Zenith's e-commerce and POS features).

Our Roles Under GDPR

Data Controller: When you create an account on Zenith, we are the Data Controller of your personal information (name, email, billing details). We determine the purposes and means of processing your data.

Data Processor: When you use Zenith to manage your boutique customers' data (orders, wishlists, accounts), you are the Data Controller and Zenith acts as a Data Processor on your behalf.

Lawful Basis for Processing

  • Contract: Processing your account information to deliver the platform service.
  • Legitimate Interests: Security monitoring, fraud prevention, and platform improvement.
  • Consent: Marketing communications (only where you have opted in).
  • Legal Obligation: Tax and financial records as required by applicable law.

Your Rights Under GDPR

Right of Access (Art. 15)
Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you.
Right to Rectification (Art. 16)
Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
Right to Erasure (Art. 17)
Request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal obligations.
Right to Portability (Art. 20)
Receive your data in a machine-readable format to transfer elsewhere.
Right to Restriction (Art. 18)
Request that we limit how we process your data in certain circumstances.
Right to Object (Art. 21)
Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.

International Data Transfers

Zenith processes data on Google Cloud infrastructure. For transfers of EU/EEA data outside the European Economic Area, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission. We ensure equivalent data protection standards for all international transfers.

Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

If you are using Zenith to process the personal data of your customers (E-commerce, CRM features), you may require a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) between your organization and Zenith. Our standard DPA is available upon request. Contact us at production@fortumars.com to request a signed DPA.

Submit a Data Subject Access Request

To exercise any of your GDPR rights, submit a Data Subject Access Request (DSAR) to production@fortumars.com with the subject line "DSAR Request." We will respond within 30 days. If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your relevant supervisory authority.
